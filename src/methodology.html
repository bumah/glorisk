Methodology

How GloRisk calculates risk

GloRisk assigns every asset a single risk score derived from price-based indicators. Each indicator is scored independently, the scores are summed, and the total determines the asset's Risk State — from Very Stable through to Critical. This page explains exactly how each step works.

The basic idea

Risk analysis can be thought of in three layers: volatility (how wildly the price moves), trend (is the price going up or down over time), and return (how much has the price actually changed). GloRisk measures all three, combining them into a single composite score.

Each indicator is scored green, amber, or red. A low score means low risk signals; a high score means multiple warning signs are active simultaneously.

● Green — low risk signal
● Amber — elevated signal
● Red — high risk signal
Risk indicators

Indicators fall into three groups: volatility, trend, and return.

Volatility
Daily Volatility
How wildly the price moves day-to-day, expressed as an annualised figure. Higher volatility means larger and more unpredictable price moves.
Volatility Spike
Recent volatility compared to the longer-term average. A rising ratio means volatility is accelerating — something is changing.
Trend
50-Day Trend
Price relative to its 50-day moving average. Positive means the price is trading above its short-term trend (bullish).
200-Day Trend
Price relative to its 200-day moving average. Positive means the long-term uptrend is intact.
Trend Direction
Whether the 50-day average is above (Golden Cross) or below (Death Cross) the 200-day average. A key signal of overall trend direction.
Return
Distance from Peak
How far the price is from its 3-year high. Large drawdowns signal sustained weakness.
30-Day Return
Total price return over the past 30 days.
12-Month Return
Total price return over the past 12 months of available data.
Position in Range
Where the price sits within its 52-week high/low band. Near the bottom signals weakness.
3-Year Growth
Compound annual growth rate over 3 years. A negative rate means the asset has lost value over time.
Composite score and Risk State

The indicator scores are summed to produce a composite score. This score is then expressed as a percentage of the maximum possible score to determine the asset's Risk State.

Using a percentage rather than a raw score means the thresholds remain stable even if the number of indicators changes in future.

Risk State
% of max risk
What it means
Very Stable
0 – 10%
Nearly all indicators are green. The asset is showing very few risk signals.
Stable
10 – 20%
Mostly green indicators with a small number of ambers. Low overall risk signal.
Unstable
20 – 50%
A mix of green, amber and red signals. Risk is elevated but not at an extreme level.
Stressed
50 – 70%
Multiple red signals are active. More than half of the maximum risk score has been reached.
Critical
70 – 100%
The majority of indicators are red. The asset is displaying widespread, severe risk signals across all three dimensions.
Data and limitations

GloRisk uses daily closing prices as its primary input. Indicators that require moving averages are computed from 50-day and 200-day simple moving averages aligned to the price history.

All calculations are based on historical price data only. GloRisk does not factor in news, on-chain data, fundamentals, sentiment, liquidity, or any qualitative information. Two assets with identical price histories will always receive identical scores.

What this score is — and isn't

The GloRisk score is a signal aggregator, not a prediction. It summarises where an asset sits across multiple price-based dimensions at a given point in time. A Critical score does not mean the price will fall further; a Very Stable score does not mean the price will rise.

High-risk scores can persist for extended periods, and assets can move from Critical to Stable very quickly. The score is most useful as a consistent, comparable snapshot — particularly when tracking how an asset's risk profile changes over time, or when comparing risk levels across a portfolio.

Future development

GloRisk currently bases its risk score entirely on price history. While this provides a consistent and objective baseline, price data alone does not capture the full picture of an asset's risk profile.

In future versions we plan to incorporate additional indicator categories — including company financials (earnings, debt, valuations) and macroeconomic factors (interest rates, inflation, broader market conditions) — to provide a more holistic and rounded risk assessment.
